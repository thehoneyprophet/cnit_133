THE BROWN BEAR

About Brown Bears

Features

Bears are mammals that belong to the family (Ursidae). The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of North America and northern Eurasia. In North America this bear is commonly referred to as a "grizzly bear". While the brown bear's range has shrunk, and it has faced local extinctions across its wide range, it remains listed as a Its current conservation status isLeast Concern.

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Bears in general vary in size, they can be as small as four feet long and weight as little as 60lbs or as big as eight feet long and weight thoudands of pounds. The brown bear is the most variable in size of modern bears. The typical size depends upon which population it is from. Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. With a range in height of 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.

Species

Brown bears are thought to have evolved from (Ursus etruscus) in Asia.

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.
